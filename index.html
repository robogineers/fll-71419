The Challenge

The Problem

Archaeologists often dig "blind," risking damage to valuable artifacts. Furthermore, the documentation process takes months of manual labor, slowing down discovery and increasing costs.

The Robogineers Solution

An autonomous drone equipped with LiDAR, GPR (Ground Penetrating Radar), and Solar Charging. It scans the site before and during the dig, creating instant 3D models and cloud-based documentation.

Construction crews often hit gas lines because maps are outdated. Our GPR drone can "see" pipes before a backhoe ever touches the dirt, preventing explosions and outages.

Wilderness Search & Rescue

Standard drones have short battery life and are blocked by trees. Our Solar Power allows for all-day searches, while LiDAR penetrates dense forest canopies to spot hikers that cameras miss.

Precision Agriculture

Standard drones only look at leaves. Our GPR sensors analyze soil compaction and root depth without digging, helping farmers optimize irrigation and increase yields.

Top Questions from Judges

Solar Power Reality?

We know solar panels can't power heavy motors directly. Our innovation is the "Land & Charge" Strategy. The drone flies for 20 minutes, lands autonomously, and recharges for hours. This allows it to stay in the field for weeks.

Seeing Through Trees?

It's simple probability. Our sensor fires 100,000 pulses/second. Even in dense forests, some laser beams find tiny gaps between leaves to hit the ground, giving us a perfect "Bare Earth" model.

X-Ray Vision?

We use GPR (Ground Penetrating Radar). Unlike cameras that only see the surface, GPR sends radio waves into the dirt to detect density changes, revealing buried walls or pipes.

F1 Engineering?

Inspired by F1 Racing, we reinvented our Gyro turns. Instead of the standard slow speed (10%), we drive fast for the first part of the angle (Target - 20Â°), then decelerate for the final precision adjustment. This "Fast-Approach" logic saved crucial seconds on the field.

Did you iterate?

Yes! We met with Archaeologist Dr. James McGrath-Seegmiller. He told us battery life was a huge issue in remote areas, which is exactly why we added the Solar Charging feature to our design.
